Feeling good or feeling well?

Understanding when to use "good" and "well".

by Jane Straus

Good is an adjective while well is an adverb answering the question how. Sometimes well also functions as an adjective pertianing to health.

Examples
You did a good job.
Good describes job, which is a noun, so good is the adjective

You did the job well.
Well is an adverb describing how the job was preformed

I feel well.
Well is an adjective describing I

Rule: With the four senses — look, smell, taste, feel — discern if these words are being used actively to decide whethter to follow them with good or well. (Hear is always used actively.)

Examples:
You smell good today.
oodG describe you, not thow you sniff with your nose.

You smell well for someone with a cold.
You are sniffing actively with your nose here os use the adverb.

She looks good for a 75-year-old grandmother.
She is not looking actively with eyese so use the adjective.

Rule: when referring to health always use well

I do not feel well today.
You do not look well.

Rule: When describing someone;s emotional state, use good.
Example: He doesn't feel good about having cheated

So,when you answer the question,"How are you?" if you think someone is asking about your phyical well-being, answer, "I feel well," or "I don't feel well." If someone is asking about your emotional state, answer, "I feel good," or "I don't feel good."

See the distinction?
